What Is 3I/ATLAS?

Key Points

  • 3I/Atlas is the third confirmed interstellar object visiting our solar system, discovered July 1st, 2025
  • NASA scientist Avi Loeb assigns 40% probability that 3I/Atlas could be of alien origin
  • The object is estimated to be 20 kilometers (12 miles) in diameter, moving at 136,000 miles per hour
  • Its orbital plane lies within 5 degrees of the ecliptic, with only 1 in 500 chance of this alignment occurring naturally
  • The object will make perihelion passage on March 16th, 2026, when Earth will be on opposite side of the sun
  • Congress is considering diverting the Juno probe to intercept the object, though fuel limitations make this unlikely
  • Atlas is outgassing approximately 3,500 tons of water per day, unusual for typical comets
  • The object's glow appears in front rather than behind it, unlike normal comets which have trailing tails
  • Its incoming trajectory from the galactic center direction made early detection extremely difficult
  • The object will pass very close to Venus (6 million miles), Mars, and potentially Jupiter during its transit
  • Its retrograde orbit makes human intercept missions nearly impossible with current chemical rocket technology
  • The timing allows for potential "clandestine reverse solar Oberth maneuver" while hidden from Earth observation
  • Previous interstellar visitors include 'Oumuamua (2017) and 2I/Borisov (2019), each with unique characteristics
  • A 12-mile asteroid impact at such velocity would likely sterilize the planet and end most higher life forms
  • The Dark Forest hypothesis suggests advanced civilizations might use disguised probes rather than broadcast their presence
  • Up to 10,000 interstellar objects may pass through our solar system regularly, mostly undetected
  • The Taurid meteor stream may be connected to historical impact events and future bombardment epochs
  • Ancient civilizations may have incorporated astronomical knowledge of these celestial events into their mythologies and religious symbolism
