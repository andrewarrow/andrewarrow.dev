The Secret History of Egypt's Black Pharaohs: What They Don't Want You to Know

Key Points

  • Nubia was a region rich in gold, semi-precious stones, ebony wood, incense, and ivory that existed since ancient times
  • The Kingdom of Kush was located south of Egypt along the Nile Valley and was known as "the land of the bow" for their skilled archers
  • Kush was also called Nubia and existed from around 2000 BCE to 350 CE, lasting over 2,000 years
  • King Piye (PA) was the most powerful Nubian pharaoh who conquered Egypt and founded the 25th Dynasty
  • Kushite priests held enormous power and could even decide when it was time for a king to die
  • Women held high status in Kush and could become leaders of the kingdom
  • King Alara was the first known Prince of Kush who unified all of upper Nubia and founded the Napatan royal dynasty
  • King Kashta greatly admired Egyptian culture and imported many artifacts, "Egyptianizing" the kingdom of Kush
  • The Kushites built pyramids (often smaller than Egyptian ones), temples, and mummified their dead like the Egyptians
  • Taharka expanded trade into the Levant and organized massive construction projects across Egypt
  • The Assyrians eventually defeated the Kushites and ended the 25th Dynasty's rule over Egypt in 656 BC
  • The sack of Thebes by Assyrians was a momentous destructive event that reverberated across history
  • Kushites continued to rule Nubia even after losing control of Egypt
  • The main crops of Kush were barley and wheat, plus cotton for clothing
  • Important trade items included incense, slaves, ivory, feathers, and wild animal hides
  • Early Kushite kings were buried on beds positioned on stone platforms in tombs below their pyramids
  • Napata and later Meroë served as political capitals of the Kushite Kingdom
  • The civilization was similar to ancient Egyptians in religion and culture, worshipping Egyptian deities like Amun and Osiris
