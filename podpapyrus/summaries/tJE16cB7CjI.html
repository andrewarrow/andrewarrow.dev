Star Trek Wrath of Khan Director on Shatner & Montalban

Key Points

  • Mark Zikree hosted an executive producer class with notable guests including Rock Neil Bannon (Farscape creator), Steven Galloway (Hollywood Reporter), and Eric Kaplan (Big Bang Theory)
  • Nicholas Meyer was a featured guest - writer/producer on Star Trek Discovery and director of Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan
  • Meyer wrote The 7% Solution, directed Time After Time and The Day After, and worked on Star Trek IV and VI
  • Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan reinvigorated and reinvented the Star Trek franchise
  • Meyer discovered that William Shatner performed better when bored rather than when "striking attitudes" as Captain Kirk
  • Getting Shatner bored required repeating takes multiple times until he let down his guard and stopped posturing
  • Ricardo Montalban was described as a great but underutilized actor who came to set letter perfect without rehearsal
  • Meyer gave Montalban a copy of Moby Dick to prepare for his role as the villain Khan
  • Montalban initially performed his six-page sequence screaming at full volume, which was too intense
  • Meyer used Lawrence Olivier's advice about not showing your "top" too early to redirect Montalban's performance
  • Montalban welcomed direction and admitted he didn't know what he was doing initially
  • Meyer's directing philosophy involves admitting ignorance and relying on expert collaborators while maintaining final creative control
  • The key moment with Shatner involved the line "Here it comes" which required multiple takes to remove heavy-handed sarcasm
  • Meyer eventually helped Shatner focus on the movie's core theme: a man getting old and losing his friends
  • Meyer believes good science fiction is always set "on planet Earth right here, right now" regardless of costumes or settings
  • The human component becomes more important than the science fiction elements in successful sci-fi storytelling
  • Meyer's approach emphasizes making actors comfortable while maintaining directorial authority
  • The collaboration between director and experienced actors requires balancing respect for their expertise with creative vision
