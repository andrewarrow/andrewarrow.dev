My dream died, and now I'm here

Key Points

  • Started university expecting physics to be about intellectual discovery and respectful scientific debate, based on naive expectations from scientist biographies
  • Discovered academia operates more like a money-making machine than a knowledge discovery institution
  • Despite excellent grades, wasn't offered a graduate position because she was female - was told to apply for women-only scholarships instead
  • Was the only woman at the institute who wasn't in administration
  • Refused to work on the department head's profitable textbook business, leading to confrontation and attempted firing
  • Academic institutions depend on overhead from research grants (15-50% of grant money) to pay staff and maintain operations
  • Researchers are kept on temporary contracts to pressure them into constantly seeking grant funding
  • Academia functions as a "paper production machine" where grant holders put their names on work done by students and postdocs
  • Success requires working on topics that are mainstream enough to get funding but slightly edgy to stand out
  • Most research projects are limited to 3-5 years, forcing focus on quick, publishable results rather than long-term discoveries
  • Academic career requires constant international moving, which is hostile to personal life and mental health
  • Women suffer more from the mobility requirements due to earlier reproductive timelines
  • Spent 5 years commuting between Frankfurt and Stockholm (different countries) to maintain both career and family
  • Developed serious mental health issues from the stress of academic life, including nervous breakdowns and constant illness
  • Learned to write grant proposals that would get funded rather than pursuing meaningful scientific questions
  • COVID-19 served as a wake-up call about life's brevity, leading to a final attempt at meaningful research funding
  • When the meaningful research wasn't funded, transitioned to YouTube to share passion for science with a wider audience
  • Successfully built a 12-person distributed team and learned business skills, finding the honest trade of knowledge for attention more satisfying than academic politics
