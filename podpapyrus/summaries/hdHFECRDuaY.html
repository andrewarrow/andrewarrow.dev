Liam Payne EXPOSES The Dark Side of One Direction | tribuune.

Key Points

  • Never got time to celebrate during One Direction - always on planes to next destination, sleeping on floors
  • One Direction was "the new wave of the Beatles" - biggest selling tour in the world for three years straight
  • Entering fame at 14 years old meant staying mentally at that age - "you leave fame the age you entered it"
  • Had no guidebook for fame - didn't know basic things like whether they could leave rooms without permission
  • The band's security approach was to "lock us in our rooms" which led to drinking problems with mini bars
  • Went through therapy and got sober for a year with help from Chip Summers
  • Recently stopped drinking again (over a month at time of interview) after seeing unflattering photos of himself
  • Had suicidal thoughts multiple times - "it's definitely been on the menu a couple of times in my life"
  • Still struggles with identity - "I still don't know who I am. I replicate different people on a daily basis"
  • Maintains closest relationship with Louis Tomlinson from the band - turns to him most for support
  • Had a supportive phone call from Harry Styles who "checked in" during a difficult time
  • Believes One Direction reunion is "a matter of when" but everyone is comfortable doing solo work currently
  • The day the band ended was both relief ("Thank Lord for that") and necessary ("I needed to stop or it would kill me")
  • Struggled with being a perfectionist in relationships and putting up barriers to protect himself
  • Fame created social anxiety and withdrawal - still waits for "work to tell me where to go"
  • Experienced severe online harassment including death threats and fake accounts claiming his mother died
  • Most disappointed in himself for "keep on hurting people" throughout his struggles
  • Emphasizes importance of supporting other young artists entering the industry with guidance he never had
