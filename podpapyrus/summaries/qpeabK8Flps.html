Destiny is Legally TRAPPED (Fast Facts)

Key Points

  • Destiny may face criminal charges related to CSAM (Child Sexual Abuse Material) allegations
  • Willie Mack reported Destiny to police after a voluntary conversation about underage material
  • Destiny voluntarily shared information about possessing explicit material involving a minor
  • Destiny claims he has a legal duty to preserve evidence for ongoing court cases
  • The person (Rose) in question has been confirmed as underage at the time the material was shared
  • Destiny's voluntary disclosure to Willie Mack likely waived attorney-client privilege
  • Law enforcement is now investigating Destiny based on the police report
  • This moves from a purely civil case to potential criminal charges
  • Destiny argues he lacked intent (mistake of fact defense) believing the person was adult
  • Most CSAM-related crimes are strict liability, meaning intent may not matter legally
  • Privilege is permanently lost when information is intentionally disclosed to third parties
  • The disclosure affects Destiny's ongoing civil lawsuit with Pixie
  • Destiny should stop giving interviews and fully disclose information to his lawyer
  • Prosecutors have discretion in deciding which charges to pursue
  • Mistake of fact and mistake of law defenses typically don't work for strict liability crimes
  • Destiny likely did not consult his lawyer before the Willie Mack conversation
  • There's a duty to preserve evidence once litigation begins (spoliation rules)
  • The situation presents both challenges and potential defense strategies for Destiny
