Russian Evolutions in the Ukraine War || Peter Zeihan

Key Points

  • Russians have shifted from mass meat assaults to small-scale infiltration tactics with 2-person teams moving incrementally
  • New Russian air strategy involves launching hundreds of drones simultaneously with missiles to saturate Ukrainian air defenses
  • Trump administration is increasing military assistance to Ukraine in increments
  • European defense industries have been scaling up for 3 years, providing more artillery shells to Ukraine
  • Russians are implementing a "Ural strategy" similar to WWII - building industrial capacity beyond enemy reach
  • Russia is now manufacturing Iranian Shahed drones domestically rather than importing fully assembled units
  • 70 years of Soviet military stockpiles are nearly exhausted after 3 years of war
  • Russians have progressed through equipment from different eras - 1980s, 1950s-60s, and now 1970s-80s vintage
  • New Russian drones include second-generation models with Nvidia Jetson chips costing $600-900 each
  • Up to two-thirds of Russian drones are cheap decoys without warheads, designed to overwhelm air defenses
  • Chinese equipment assembly has moved from China to Russia, complicating supply chain disruption efforts
  • Russians coordinate drone swarms with missile attacks and glide bombs for maximum infrastructure damage
  • Russian military is transitioning from artillery-focused to drone-based warfare due to artillery losses
  • New drones have primitive decision-making capabilities, representing a technological advancement
  • Russian attacks now target civilian infrastructure and power systems in urban areas
  • The strategy creates shifting no-man's lands where small teams can exploit soft spots for larger assaults
  • Any drone strikes on schools are automatic war crimes since targets must be pre-programmed
  • This represents the most significant shift in Russian war strategy since the conflict began
