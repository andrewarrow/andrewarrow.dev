Kilmar Abrego Garcia taken into ICE custody, faces deportation to Uganda

Key Points

  • Kilmar Abrego Garcia is currently in ICE custody after showing up for an appointment at a Baltimore field office
  • US immigration officials have threatened to deport the Maryland resident to Uganda after he declined a plea deal
  • Large crowd chanted in support as he entered the ICE facility in Baltimore
  • ICE threatened to deport Abrego Garcia (a Salvadoran national) to Uganda after he declined a deal to be sent to Costa Rica in exchange for pleading guilty to federal smuggling charges
  • He spent the weekend with his family after release from a Tennessee jail where he'd been held since June
  • The Trump administration brought him back from El Salvador on criminal charges after mistakenly deporting him to his home country in March
  • His case has become a symbol of the administration's immigration crackdown
  • The Trump administration alleges he is an MS-13 gang member, human trafficker, and that he beat his wife - allegations he denies
  • CASA, an immigration advocacy group, is championing his case
  • His attorneys have filed a lawsuit to try to prevent his deportation for a second time
  • The lawsuit triggered a court order automatically pausing any deportation actions for 2 days
  • Abrego Garcia has been living in Maryland for years after entering the US illegally
  • He was stopped for speeding in 2022
  • The government claims he was smuggling people because he had eight passengers in his vehicle
  • He prayed with his family and hugged his wife before entering ICE custody
  • His message to supporters was translated by a CASA representative
  • There are opposing views on his case - some calling it propaganda while others believe he should be imprisoned
  • The case involves multiple deportation attempts and legal proceedings across different states
