LEAK: 4'11" Teenage Ninja Makes HISTORY | American Ninja Warrior | NBC

Key Points

  • Jason Behrends is 15 years old, 4'11" tall, and weighs only 95 pounds
  • He's nicknamed "Biggie Smalls" and has "play big" written on his shirt
  • Jason designed his own logo featuring his glasses and hat
  • He's from Iowa and part of a strong ninja community there
  • His father Scott runs Ninja U, one of Iowa's premier ninja gyms
  • Jason previously competed on "Ninja Junior" before moving to the main course
  • He has siblings (brother Ben and sister) who support him from the sidelines
  • Despite his small size, he completed the Log Runner obstacle with ease
  • He successfully navigated the Warped Wall without difficulty
  • Jason completed the Salmon Ladder obstacle effortlessly
  • He mastered the Corkscrew obstacle with 540 degrees of rotation
  • The Dungeon obstacle was particularly challenging due to his light weight
  • He barely moved the heavy board that weighs more than him
  • Jason overcame the physics disadvantage through pure determination and technique
  • He successfully transferred between boards weighing about 60 pounds each
  • Jason became the smallest and lightest athlete ever to complete an extended obstacle course
  • He was the first finisher of the night
  • His performance moved spectators and family members to tears of joy
