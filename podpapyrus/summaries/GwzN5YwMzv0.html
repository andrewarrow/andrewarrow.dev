Does the Past Still Exist?

Key Points

  • Time is one of the biggest mysteries in both existence and physics
  • Einstein fundamentally changed our understanding of time, showing it differs greatly from our perception
  • Hermann Minkowski first suggested treating time as a dimension rather than just a parameter
  • Maxwell's equations make more sense when space and time are combined into four-dimensional spacetime
  • Einstein's special relativity is based on four key assumptions: speed of light is finite and constant, nothing goes faster than light, and all observer viewpoints are equally valid
  • The concept of "now" is observer-dependent, not universal - different observers have different definitions of simultaneity
  • Einstein's thought experiment with mirrors demonstrates how observers moving at different velocities disagree on what happens "now"
  • The relativity of simultaneity means your "now" is not the same as someone else's "now"
  • These effects are only noticeable when relative velocities approach the speed of light
  • Any two causally disconnected events can happen simultaneously for some observer
  • The block universe theory suggests all times exist equally - past, present, and future all exist "now"
  • If you accept that distant events exist "now," then logically all events must exist simultaneously
  • Alternative approaches include only accepting local existence or defining arbitrary time slices, but these have problems
  • Quantum mechanics doesn't change the block universe concept because it's still compatible with special relativity
  • The measurement update in quantum mechanics happens faster than light but can't be observed, so there's no contradiction
  • Some argue quantum indeterminacy means the future doesn't exist yet, but this argument still applies to the past
  • Based on current physics knowledge, the past exists in the same way as the present
  • Special relativity is experimentally well-confirmed, making alternative theories that contradict it unlikely to be correct
