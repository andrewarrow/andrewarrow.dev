DANIELLE PANABAKER Talks About the Highs and Lows of Parenting as a Working Actor

Key Points

  • Having two children versus one parent creates challenging dynamics where you need patience from the kids
  • Parenting requires working on patience as an ongoing skill that can always be improved
  • Having kids brings out your deepest, darkest thoughts but you have to push through and handle it
  • Parenting is exhausting, challenging, and much harder than ever imagined
  • Despite the difficulties, there's something magical about having children
  • Children are like little versions of you but also better and unique
  • Kids constantly reveal new aspects of themselves to their parents
  • The love for children is even deeper than the love for beloved pets
  • Past childhood experiences can create fear about becoming a parent
  • Fear of being too hard on yourself as a parent is common
  • Perfectionism and fear of messing up can prevent people from wanting children
  • The reality is that you will make mistakes as a parent - it's inevitable
  • Setting a good example for children is one of the most important things you can do
  • Apologizing when you mess up is crucial for healthy parenting
  • Making things right after mistakes shows children how to handle their own errors
  • Parenting involves accepting that you're not perfect and will have failures
  • The emotional investment in children creates vulnerability but also deep meaning
  • Parenting forces personal growth and self-reflection about your own upbringing
