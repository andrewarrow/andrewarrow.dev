Why Are All Movies Just Remakes and Sequels Now? | Insider Justine Bateman

Key Points

  • Hollywood was established by risk-takers willing to create something from nothing and earn uncertain income
  • Tech companies changed the industry by treating films and series as "content" for their platforms rather than art
  • The industry lost its "north star" of excellence and outstanding work as the primary goal
  • Streaming services caused studios to chase volume over quality in content production
  • Studios began behaving more like corporations, reacting to the loudest voices on social media
  • Fear mode led to an abundance of reboots, remakes, sequels, and creative/financial timidity
  • The financial ecosystem became detrimental to filmmakers, crew, and everyone involved in production
  • Good actors are now delivering "phoned in" performances in poorly scripted and directed projects
  • It became shameful to prioritize money over artistic merit, but that standard has eroded
  • Quality films and series struggle to get proper audience support and promotion
  • The industry eliminated most legitimate film critics, replacing them with content summarizers
  • Finding actual quality films requires "pawing through" a "giant Costco" of mediocre content
  • There's no clear leadership or vision guiding the industry anymore
  • People seek connection through conflict ("intimacy through injury") rather than genuine relationships
  • The entertainment business attracts people with very unique and specific skill sets
  • Industry veterans who started as assistants now run major companies and studios
  • The 1990s and 2000s represented a golden era of high-quality storytelling in cinema
  • Current good films are produced at about one-third the rate they were decades ago
