Jennette McCurdy - “I’m Glad My Mom Died” | The Daily Show

Key Points

  • Jennette McCurdy wrote a book titled "I'm Glad My Mom Died" exploring complicated parent-child relationships
  • She doesn't hate her mom but sees her as a complicated, nuanced person who was at times abusive
  • McCurdy felt relieved when her mom died, a feeling society doesn't accept discussing openly
  • Her mother always wanted to be an actress and put McCurdy in acting at age 6 to live vicariously through her
  • McCurdy was a child star on iCarly, appearing happy on camera while suffering trauma behind the scenes
  • Child star exploitation is more common than people want to admit, based on her audition experiences
  • She uses humor as a coping mechanism to process trauma, making difficult stories more readable
  • McCurdy spent 10 years in therapy working through her relationship with her mother
  • Her therapist advised dropping forgiveness as a goal, calling it "still trying to do your mom's work"
  • Society puts mothers on pedestals, making it taboo to criticize them unlike fathers
  • McCurdy was willing to face backlash because she felt the message was worth sharing
  • The book deals with the conflict between the idea of a "mom" versus one's actual mother
  • She's returned to entertainment as a director, writer, and podcaster - behind the camera this time
  • McCurdy worries about being sucked back into toxic entertainment industry patterns
  • She now has better tools and discernment to avoid getting lost in the industry whirlwind
  • Therapy and grounding practices help her stay connected to reality in entertainment environments
  • The book's success comes from addressing the taboo topic of unloving or harmful parental relationships
  • McCurdy trusts herself not to repeat past patterns because she's developed better coping mechanisms
