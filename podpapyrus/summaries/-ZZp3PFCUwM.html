There Are Some ‘Breaking Bad’ Scenes Betsy Brandt STILL Can’t Watch | The Rich Eisen Show

Key Points

  • Anna Gunn (Betsy Brandt) and Dean Norris met in the waiting room while auditioning for Breaking Bad, both thinking it was a comedy
  • The Breaking Bad pilot was described as "hands down the best pilot I've ever read" by Betsy Brandt
  • Bryan Cranston wasn't cast yet when Betsy went to her auditions (she auditioned 2-3 times)
  • Vince Gilligan is described as a "creative genius" and "one of the best people you'll ever meet" despite creating dark content
  • The show didn't gain massive popularity until after season 3, when it hit Netflix and people could binge-watch
  • Scripts were redacted and kept top secret to prevent leaks, especially in later seasons
  • Someone not involved with the show once found a script, highlighting security concerns
  • The cast would take a "class picture" together every year
  • The toughest scene for Betsy Brandt was when Marie slapped Skyler, ending their close relationship
  • Betsy Brandt hasn't watched some scenes because they're too emotionally difficult for her
  • The scene where Hank discovers Walter is Heisenberg while on the toilet was particularly impactful
  • Bryan Cranston's most difficult scene was watching Jane (Kristen Ritter's character) die
  • Bryan Cranston was found crying after filming the Jane death scene
  • Actors had to "hold it together" while filming emotional scenes, then process the emotions afterward
  • The show continues to gain new fans who are binge-watching it years later
  • Sony and AMC were the networks that put money behind the unusual concept
  • AMC was willing to keep the show on air despite initially modest viewership numbers
  • The awkward guacamole scene was particularly stressful for the actress, giving her chest pains during filming
