What A Quarterback Battle Is REALLY Like

Key Points

  • Lost quarterback battles to multiple players who became NFL starters (Cody Kesler, Sam Darnold, Ben DiNucci, Kenny Pickett)
  • Had five different head coaches during college career, providing extensive perspective on QB competitions
  • Being a "vibes guy" with good personality and EQ matters significantly in QB battles
  • Vocal leadership and locker room credibility give major advantages, especially for veteran players
  • Championship teams are typically player-led, which favors experienced quarterbacks over raw talent
  • Only one starting position available means losers sit on sideline with rarely another opportunity
  • Talent gives privilege and allows players to get away with more, but there's always a limit
  • You can execute perfectly and still lose due to variables outside your control (coverage, play calls, personnel)
  • Media coverage and public scrutiny make QB battles unique compared to regular job applications
  • Consequences are severe - losing means transferring across country or potentially giving up the sport entirely
  • Best coaching approach is complete transparency - telling all quarterbacks the decision simultaneously
  • Avoid checking social media and beat writer coverage during competitions to maintain mental health
  • Don't rely on practice statistics alone - they ignore crucial variables and context
  • Don't call it a "competition" if the decision is already 80% made - be honest about the situation
  • Transfer portal has changed everything - players won't wait around if they don't see a path to starting
  • Modern offensive schemes are simpler, allowing younger quarterbacks to compete faster than 15 years ago
  • Coaches now avoid naming starters after spring ball to prevent transfers in May window
  • Current notable QB battles: Alabama (Ty Simpson vs Austin Mack vs Keenan Russell), Cal (Devin Brown vs freshman), Colorado (Kaden Salter vs Julian Lewis), Michigan (Bryce Underwood vs Mikey Keane), Ohio State (Julian Sayin vs Lincoln Kienholz)
