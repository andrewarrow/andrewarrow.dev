Pod Strickland Patreon Preview Episode 520 feat. Drew Steele: Knicks' Best Moves of the 2000s

Key Points

  • This is episode 520 of Pod Strickland podcast, hosted by Shwony Poo with guest Drew (aka Doug)
  • They are drafting the New York Knicks' 10 best moves from the 2000s (2000-2010 seasons)
  • The Strickland has a YouTube channel, merchandise store, Patreon with multiple tiers ($6, $9, $15, $30, $50, $100), and Discord community
  • First pick: Danilo Gallinari - drafted by Knicks, later traded in the Carmelo Anthony deal
  • Gallinari was considered the best Knicks draft pick of the decade, known for drawing fouls at an elite level
  • Eric Gordon (picked 7th) and Brook Lopez (picked 10th) were potentially better options than Gallinari (picked 6th)
  • Second pick: 2007 trade acquiring Zack Randolph - traded Steve Francis, Channing Frye, and 2008 second-round pick
  • Randolph became a 2-time All-Star and All-NBA third team player after leaving the Knicks
  • The Knicks were too impatient with Randolph, trading him before seeing his full potential
  • Randolph's situation parallels the Julius Randle acquisition - undervalued power forward with potential
  • 2005 draft moves mentioned: acquiring Nate Robinson (21st pick) and David Lee through separate trades
  • Nate Robinson trade: Knicks sent Kurt Thomas and 54th pick to Phoenix for Quentin Richardson and Robinson's draft rights
  • Richardson was younger (age 25) than Thomas (age 33) but had shooting limitations inside the arc
  • The Knicks effectively moved up 33 draft spots (from 54th to 21st pick) in the Robinson trade
  • Kurt Thomas is mentioned as deserving consideration for the top 10 list
  • The podcast discusses how the Knicks' poor team construction prevented success with acquired talent
  • Robinson won a dunk contest, adding entertainment value beyond basketball performance
  • The trades showed occasional smart asset management during an otherwise difficult decade for the franchise
