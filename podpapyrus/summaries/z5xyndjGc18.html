MLB Player Goes Undercover vs. High School Team

Key Points

  • Trevor May is a former MLB pitcher who played 9 years and threw over 100 mph
  • He went undercover against a 17U high school baseball team
  • The team was told they'd face an average pitcher for free gear prizes
  • Prize structure: contact in play wins a bat ($400-450 Rawlings icons), actual hits win bat plus Bruce Bolt batting gloves
  • Trevor wore his Oakland A's uniform and warmed up away from the field
  • The team consists of 16-17 year old players with D1, D2, D3 college commits
  • Trevor is currently training to reach 100 mph again in his "Road to 100" series
  • During warmups, Trevor hit 88 mph in recent bullpen sessions
  • The first pitcher (Dan) threw in the low 80s and gave up a hit immediately
  • Trevor entered as a "reliever" to surprise the unsuspecting players
  • Trevor consistently threw 90-93 mph fastballs during the session
  • He threw effective sliders and changeups to complement his fastball
  • Two players managed to get hits off Trevor and won prizes
  • One player made contact and earned a bat prize
  • Trevor spent 20 minutes after pitching answering questions from the kids
  • The video promotes Trevor's YouTube channel and "Road to 100" series
  • A giveaway offers MLB jerseys to subscribers who comment their Instagram
  • The experience showcased the significant skill gap between MLB and high school baseball
