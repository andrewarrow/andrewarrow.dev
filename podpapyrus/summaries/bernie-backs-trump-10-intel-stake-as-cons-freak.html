Bernie BACKS Trump 10% Intel Stake As Cons FREAK

Key Points

  • Trump announced the US now owns 10% of Intel valued at approximately $11 billion, paying nothing by converting Chips Act grants into equity
  • Intel is one of the only major US semiconductor manufacturers that creates their own fabrication facilities (fabs)
  • Nvidia and AMD don't manufacture chips - they only design them while TSMC (Taiwan) manufactures over 80-90% of their products
  • Over 90% of advanced chip manufacturing happens in Taiwan, creating a critical geopolitical vulnerability for the US
  • Building new semiconductor fabs takes 5-10 years and requires massive capital investment
  • State-controlled competitors like Samsung (Korea), TSMC (Taiwan), and SMIC (China) don't prioritize profit and can subsidize operations to dominate markets
  • Bernie Sanders endorsed the principle that taxpayers deserve returns on government investments in private companies
  • The deal converts $8.9 million in previously awarded but unpaid Chips Act grants into equity at $20.47 per share
  • Critics argue this represents government socialism and interference in free markets
  • Supporters argue national security requires government control over critical industries like semiconductors
  • During WWII, the US War Production Board controlled manufacturing output and seized facilities when necessary for national security
  • 48% of TSMC's initial startup capital came from the Taiwanese government, demonstrating state capitalism's effectiveness
  • Morris Chang learned semiconductor manufacturing at Texas Instruments before founding TSMC in Taiwan
  • China's state capitalism model has lifted millions out of poverty and created competitive industries like BYD automotive
  • The US has moved from mass production capabilities to highly specialized manufacturing, similar to Germany and Japan in WWII
  • America currently lacks domestic manufacturing for critical industries including pharmaceuticals, oil refineries, and nuclear reactors
  • The equity stake gives the US government ownership without voting control or direct operational influence over Intel
  • Concerns exist that Trump may use government control to reward allies and punish political opponents rather than serve national interests
