I’ve Been Back On Every "Premium" Cruise Line. Here’s What I Found

Key Points

  • Premium cruise lines carry almost 20% of cruise passengers annually and include Princess, Holland America, Cunard, Celebrity, and Virgin Voyages
  • Premium ships are midsize to large (2,000-3,500 passengers) with more space and crew per guest than mainstream lines, offering less crowded experiences
  • Premium lines focus on classic cruise experiences with enrichment programs, trivia, dancing, live music, and production shows rather than resort-style attractions
  • Fares are roughly twice the price of mainstream resort lines but still include many add-on charges like Wi-Fi, gratuities, and specialty dining
  • Princess Cruises is the largest premium line with 17 ships featuring medallion-based wearable technology for seamless onboard experiences
  • Princess offers the most active daily program among premium lines and is best for busy classic cruise experiences and multigenerational families
  • Celebrity Cruises positions itself as the most luxurious premium line with "modern luxury" and status-based amenities like The Retreat for suite guests
  • Celebrity's Edge-class ships offer a more youthful, trendy experience while Solstice and Millennium classes provide traditional cruising
  • Holland America focuses on traditional cruising with the best destination range and longer, more immersive journeys in the premium category
  • Holland America is an Alaska expert with over 70 years of experience and operates trains and lodges in addition to ships
  • Cunard offers the most formal and traditional British cruising experience with class-based dining structure and strict dress codes
  • Cunard provides unique heritage routes including regular transatlantic crossings and annual World Voyages on all ships
  • Virgin Voyages is the only 18+ adult-only premium line with edgy entertainment, party events, and extensive wellness programs
  • Virgin includes all food, gratuities, and Wi-Fi in base fares with no specialty dining charges and offers exceptional cuisine quality
  • Virgin Voyages has the highest passenger ratings (4.1/5) among premium lines but limited destinations focused on Mediterranean and Caribbean
  • Premium lines cruise more worldwide destinations compared to mainstream lines that primarily focus on Caribbean, Mediterranean, and Alaska
  • All premium lines maintain classic cruise ship designs without water slides, climbing walls, or other resort-style attractions
  • The average passenger age varies by line, with Virgin around 50 years old, Princess attracting younger families, and Cunard appealing to passengers 60s and above
