Zach Braff Thought “Scrubs” Was Too Good To Be True | Conan O'Brien Needs A Friend

Key Points

  • Started as a child actor with a CBS pilot at age 14 that didn't get picked up
  • First movie role was in Manhattan Murder Mystery with Woody Allen, Diane Keaton, and Anjelica Huston at age 18
  • Chose to attend Northwestern film school instead of pursuing acting momentum after the movie role
  • Moved to LA and waited tables while pursuing acting career
  • Got cast in Scrubs in 2000 at age 24
  • Experienced heightened anxiety between getting cast and production starting on Scrubs
  • Used the waiting period before Scrubs filming to write the first draft of Garden State
  • The Scrubs pilot felt like it was written specifically for him
  • Had immediate chemistry with creator Bill Lawrence due to shared sense of humor
  • Lived so frugally that pilot money could last him years (burritos for lunch, PB&J for dinner)
  • Quit his waiting tables job after getting cast, never had to return to it
  • Met Donald Faison at the table read and it was "love at first sight" as friends
  • Their real-life friendship directly influenced the JD and Turk characters on screen
  • Bill Lawrence would incorporate their weekend stories into the show's scripts
  • The show depicted an unusual portrayal of male friendship with constant hugging and "I love you"s
  • Bill Lawrence was making a commentary on masculinity through their bromance relationship
  • The characters were written as straight men who loved each other in all other ways
  • Their off-camera antics and friendship fed back into the show's development
