The Shocking Truth About Nigeria’s Slave Trade History!

Key Points

  • Badagri, off the coast of Lagos, Nigeria, was a major hub for the transatlantic slave trade
  • The Brazilian Barracoon compound contained 40 rooms, each holding 40 slaves in inhumane conditions
  • Slaves were traded for European goods like umbrellas (1 umbrella = 40 human beings) and cannons (1 cannon = 200 slaves)
  • Heavy chains weighing several pounds were worn around slaves' necks and ankles at all times
  • Slaves had extremely limited ventilation through tiny windows and were forced to drink from communal pots without cups
  • The "Point of No Return" was where slaves boarded ships, marking their final departure from Africa
  • Slavery already existed in Africa before Europeans arrived - Africans were enslaving other Africans through tribal warfare
  • African chiefs and local leaders actively participated as facilitators in the slave trade, selling their own people
  • The Portuguese were the first to establish the trade, followed by the English with ships like "Jesus of Lubec"
  • Slave ships could carry 600-700 people with crews of only 25-30, leading to frequent revolts
  • A "memory loss well" was built by African chiefs, believed to make slaves forget their past for 3-4 months
  • Slaves had to walk around a "tree of memory loss" (women 7 times, men 9 times) before boarding ships
  • The "Ark of Departure" monument symbolized the final passage under which slaves walked in chains
  • Many major structures in America, including the White House, were built by enslaved Africans
  • The historical sites in Badagri are poorly maintained and not properly preserved
  • A tunnel monument project has been under construction for over 10 years without completion
  • The slave trade involved both forced capture and voluntary selling by African leaders seeking power and wealth
  • This history demonstrates how Africans participated in betraying their own people, a pattern that continues today according to the guide
