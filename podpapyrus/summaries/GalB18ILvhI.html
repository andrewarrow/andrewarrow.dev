Issues on Set of a Hit Show When It Starts to Grow (with DANIELLE PANABAKER)

  • For Smallville, the hierarchy was "Tom, everyone else" but the speaker fought against this

  • Contract negotiations were the worst experience of the speaker's life
  • The speaker almost walked away from the show due to negotiation difficulties
  • The problems were with studio executives, not the creative team or cast
  • The speaker believes in fair compensation when a company is doing well and you contribute significantly
  • The speaker's life was miserable for two years because they allowed the situation to affect them
  • The core story of Smallville was Tom becoming Superman and the speaker becoming Lex Luthor
  • Being a team player and having good work habits doesn't always lead to fair treatment
  • Bad behavior sometimes gets rewarded while good behavior goes unrecognized
  • TV contracts typically lock actors in for six years with limited negotiating power
  • Renegotiation points are crucial moments for making career decisions
  • Once you accept a deal, you must own that decision and not remain bitter about it
