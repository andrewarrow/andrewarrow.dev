What It's Like Having No Inner Monologue and Aphantasia (a blind mind's eye)

Key Points

  • The speaker discovered at age 40 that most people see mental images in their heads, which they cannot do
  • They have aphantasia - the inability to create mental visual imagery
  • They also lack an inner monologue and their mind is generally quiet
  • They cannot visualize loved ones, including their own children and husband
  • They read 150 books per year but cannot see any mental imagery while reading
  • They cannot recall smells or tastes when thinking about them
  • Dreams are experienced as feelings rather than visual images
  • They have excellent memory for events and actions but struggle with specific visual details
  • All memories feel equally vivid/vague regardless of when they occurred
  • They can get songs stuck in their head, sung by the original artists' voices
  • The discovery was emotionally devastating and led to an existential crisis
  • They describe their thinking like an old computer that outputs information cards rather than visual displays
  • Speech feels automatic like muscle memory rather than being consciously constructed
  • They encourage others with aphantasia to grieve the loss and seek support
  • The star visualization test isn't perfect - visualization exists on a spectrum
  • Some people with aphantasia still have inner monologue, while others don't
  • There's a supportive Facebook group for people with aphantasia
  • They compare their memory to navigating a foggy city at night - knowing where things are without seeing them clearly
