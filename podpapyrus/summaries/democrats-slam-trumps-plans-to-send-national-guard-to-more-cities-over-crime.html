Democrats slam Trump's plans to send National Guard to more cities over crime

Key Points

  • Democrats are pushing back against President Trump's plan to send National Guard troops to more cities
  • Targeted cities include Chicago, New York, and Baltimore
  • State and local leaders claim they have crime under control
  • Democratic leaders are calling Trump's threats to deploy troops in American cities unconstitutional
  • Trump and Maryland Governor Wes Moore engaged in a war of words over crime issues
  • Trump posted on social media threatening to send troops to Maryland if Governor Moore needs help
  • National Guard troops are already being deployed in Washington DC
  • Governor Moore criticized the troop deployment as "purely performative"
  • Moore invited Trump to visit Baltimore, where officials say violent crime is down
  • Trump has repeatedly blasted Baltimore and other Democratic-run cities as crime-ridden
  • Chicago is reportedly next on Trump's list for potential military deployment
  • The Pentagon is drawing up plans for possible military deployment in Chicago
  • Illinois Governor JB Pritzker says Trump is trying to manufacture a crisis
  • Illinois did not request federal help with crime issues
  • Chicago's mayor reports that violent crime is down in the city
  • Local officials argue the president lacks constitutional authority to federalize National Guard in their states
  • Trump has more power to deploy troops in Washington DC than in other states
  • Presidents can federalize state national guards during emergencies, as Trump did in Los Angeles in June
