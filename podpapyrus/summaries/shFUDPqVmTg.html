I was asked to keep this confidential

Key Points

  • A physicist received a confidential email 7 years ago from someone at a top US institution after publishing a comment about losing trust in scientific self-correction
  • The email author admits to creating "pretty useless stuff" - old research "refurbished with a couple of new bells and whistles" but justifies it because "people buy this and it helps them to get grants"
  • The email describes academic physics as a "bubble" that employs thousands of people and their families, preventing them from "dying from hunger"
  • The author claims taxpayers "have zero idea that elementary particles exist" and pay for "something cool, some new crazy hype" from public funds
  • The DUNE experiment, costing billions in public funds, is criticized as unable to answer why the universe contains more matter than antimatter - calling this a "pseudo problem"
  • The US government spent another $2 billion on a particle collider at Brookhaven to measure quark and gluon distributions in heavy ions, primarily to keep physicists employed
  • The email reveals a system where "obedient idiots remain" in academia while "bright intelligent persons with independent ways of thinking" get kicked out
  • Quality criteria changes that would demonstrate the uselessness of someone's work "will have zero chance of approval"
  • The problem exists not just in high-energy physics but "in all other areas" of academia
  • The email author warns that criticizing the system could affect redistribution of funds away from high-energy physics
  • Some physicists have "families and young children" and academia is "the only way to get US Visa" for some international researchers
  • The bubble of useless research "is not a problem that can be fixed from inside the community"
  • The narrator believes "the only way to fix the problem is to stop paying them" and predicts this will happen
  • The email demonstrates extreme condescension toward taxpayers who fund academic research
  • Many physicists allegedly know about nonsense research but "keep their mouth shut" due to career concerns
  • The narrator accuses physicists of "lying to the people who pay you" and being "cowards without a shred of scientific integrity"
  • The system protects researchers through job security while producing little genuine scientific value
  • The narrator changed from calling it "bullshit research" to "nonsense research" because YouTube flags the former as profanity
