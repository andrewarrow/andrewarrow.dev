At 88, Robert Redford Names The Seven Actors He Hated THE MOST

Key Points

  • Robert Redford, at 88, has revealed which actors made his work unbearable during his decades-long career
  • Gene Hackman was described as "a sledgehammer in a violin shop" - brilliant but impossible to work with
  • Redford and Hackman clashed during "Downhill Racer" over Hackman's volatile temperament and refusal to leave his trailer
  • James Woods was blacklisted by Redford after challenging him on Vietnam War views during an audition
  • Redford's casting notes for Woods contained just two words: "Never ever"
  • Dustin Hoffman's improvisational style during "All the President's Men" frustrated Redford's meticulous approach
  • Tom Cruise treated "Lions for Lambs" like a corporate brand campaign rather than a political drama
  • Redford found Cruise's focus on image consultants and market positioning instead of character development unbearable
  • Faye Dunaway was called "a storm in heels" during "Three Days of The Condor" due to her demanding perfectionism
  • Robert Duvall's jazz-like improvisational approach clashed with Redford's symphonic precision on "The Natural"
  • Paul Newman, despite their iconic on-screen partnership, kept distance and became competitive rather than collaborative
  • Redford felt Newman pushed him aside during "The Sting" by exerting more producer control
  • Many of these working relationships ended permanently with no reunions or second collaborations
  • Redford preferred collaboration, momentum, and carefully constructed emotional arcs in his films
  • Political and philosophical differences often contributed to these professional conflicts
  • Despite public perception of close friendships, many relationships were more complicated behind the scenes
  • Redford navigated Hollywood storms quietly without scandal or public spectacle
  • These revelations show that even Hollywood's most composed stars carried scars from difficult collaborations
