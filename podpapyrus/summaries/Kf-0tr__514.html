KICK IS F*CKED | The Kyle Kulinski Show

Key Points

  • Kick is a streaming platform that positions itself as a "wild west" alternative to Twitch with more lenient rules
  • A group of French streamers tortured a vulnerable man named Jean Pormanov to death during a live 10-day marathon stream
  • Jean Pormanov was forced to ingest toxic substances and was physically abused on camera
  • The victim tried to fight back and expressed wanting to leave, saying "they're not letting me leave" to his concerned mother
  • Kick's CEO Ed Craven was the fifth top donator to the stream, actively funding the torture with $150
  • Viewers donated money specifically to encourage more acts of violence and humiliation
  • The CEO was watching and financially supporting the torture while his platform failed to intervene or ban the stream
  • This incident represents a "race to the bottom" when platforms have minimal content moderation
  • The narrator argues this changed his perspective on free speech absolutism and the need for regulations
  • Major internet celebrities like Aiden Ross stream on Kick, and Mr. Beast appeared on the platform shortly before this incident
  • The incident raises questions about whether society is becoming more evil or if technology is just exposing existing darkness
  • Similar disturbing incidents have occurred on streaming platforms, including someone freezing to death in Russia
  • The narrator believes the perpetrators should face manslaughter and kidnapping charges
  • There's concern that accountability may not follow due to current justice system failures
  • The incident is connected to broader societal moral decay and normalization of cruelty
  • Underground content like "Faces of Death" has moved into mainstream platforms with celebrity endorsement
  • The narrator argues this represents a fundamental shift in values where being cruel became virtuous
  • The story highlights the need for government intervention and platform responsibility in preventing such content
