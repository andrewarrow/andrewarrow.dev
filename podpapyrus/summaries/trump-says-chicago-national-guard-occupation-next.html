Trump Says CHICAGO NATIONAL GUARD OCCUPATION Next

Key Points

  • Trump is expanding National Guard deployments from LA and DC to Chicago next, with plans for other cities
  • Pentagon has been planning military deployment to Chicago for weeks, involving thousands of National Guard members
  • 1,700 National Guardsmen will mobilize in 19 Republican-governed states to assist DHS with immigration crackdown
  • DC troops will now be allowed to carry firearms, a change from their initial orders
  • Legal framework differs between deployments - DC has federal authority while Chicago would be more like the contested LA model
  • Illinois Governor Pritzker strongly opposes the deployment, stating no emergency warrants federal intervention
  • Chicago Mayor Brandon Johnson has an 80% disapproval rating and was elected on a progressive platform
  • Quality of life crime and homelessness are major issues in blue cities, contributing to population loss
  • Violent crime has actually decreased in many cities including DC (lowest in 30 years) and Baltimore (lowest in 50 years)
  • The deployment is seen as political domination and show of force rather than genuine crime-fighting effort
  • Masked, unidentified agents refusing to show credentials have led to dangerous incidents with homeowners
  • Trump directly threatened Maryland Governor Wes Moore, offering to send troops to Baltimore
  • Moore opposed deployment citing it's unsustainable, unscalable, unconstitutional, and disrespectful to National Guard
  • Deployment costs over $1 million per day and raises serious constitutional questions under Posse Comitatus Act
  • Democratic politicians like Newsom and Moore are gaining national profiles by publicly opposing Trump
  • Polling shows 51% oppose DC police takeover and National Guard deployment, matching partisan divisions
  • 57% believe Trump is using deployments to distract from other issues, while 51% think it's necessary for crime
  • The debate reflects broader tensions over gun laws, social safety nets, and different approaches to urban governance
