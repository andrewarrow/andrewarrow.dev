I'm HITCHHIKING from Portugal to Australia! (Season 2, Ep 1.1)

Key Points

  • Hitchhiking journey from Portugal to Australia with current focus on crossing Turkey
  • Challenges of hitchhiking in big cities versus smaller villages and gas stations
  • Social energy management is the biggest struggle - being an extreme introvert while constantly meeting new people
  • Turkish license plate system: first two digits indicate region (34 for Istanbul, 35 for Izmir, 60 for Bursa)
  • Villages and rural areas have much friendlier, more helpful people than cities
  • Sleeping in mosques as accommodation - researched online to ensure respectfulness
  • Safety concerns in poor urban areas of Izmir - trusted gut instinct to leave quickly
  • Hitchhiking has shown the world is much safer than most people think
  • Aggressive stray dogs can be a real danger in rural areas
  • Weather challenges including storms and cold temperatures affecting camping plans
  • Gas stations are ideal hitchhiking spots with better success rates than city streets
  • Financial constraints - avoiding expensive hostels (€16+) when possible
  • Technology needs for content creation - requiring places to charge computer for editing
  • Cultural interactions and language barriers throughout Turkey
  • Sleeping outdoors safety tip: keep valuables (passport, phone, wallet) inside sleeping bag near feet
  • Turkish people's helpfulness - even strangers going out of their way to assist
  • Navigation challenges when rides don't go to intended destinations
  • Physical challenges of carrying 20kg+ backpack plus food while walking long distances
