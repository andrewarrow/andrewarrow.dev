Phil Collins, In The Air Tonight & That Drum Fill | New British Canon

Key Points

  • Phil Collins dominated the 1980s both as a solo artist and Genesis frontman, selling as many records as U2 or Bruce Springsteen
  • His solo career began after his marriage to Andrea Bertterelli fell apart during Genesis's extensive touring in 1978
  • Phil Collins replaced Peter Gabriel as Genesis frontman in 1975 after Gabriel left due to exhaustion from the rock lifestyle
  • "In the Air Tonight" was written during Collins's darkest period in 1979 while dealing with his divorce and isolation
  • The iconic drum sound was created using SSL desk compression and gating technology at Townhouse Studio 2
  • The famous drum fill in "In the Air Tonight" was a late addition suggested by Atlantic Records' Ahmet Ertegun
  • Collins used a Roland CR-78 drum machine for the song's relentless, mechanized beat that creates the dark atmosphere
  • Face Value (1981) spent 270 weeks in UK charts - essentially half of the 1980s decade
  • Collins was the only musician to perform at both London and Philadelphia Live Aid concerts, traveling by Concorde
  • "Against All Odds" was originally written in 1979 as a Face Value B-side before becoming a movie theme song
  • Collins sold 120 million albums worldwide during the 1980s (excluding Genesis sales)
  • His gated reverb drum sound became the defining sound of 1980s music production
  • Critics attacked Collins for constantly discussing his divorce and his appearance, with journalist Julie Burchill calling him "the ugliest man since George Orwell"
  • An urban legend falsely claimed "In the Air Tonight" was about Collins witnessing a drowning, which Eminem referenced in "Stan"
  • US R&B and hip-hop artists heavily sample Collins's work, with Ice-T famously defending his Phil Collins collection
  • Collins appeared on both film and TV, including Miami Vice, and won an Oscar for "You'll Be in My Heart" from Disney's Tarzan
  • Genesis evolved from 23-minute progressive rock epics to more accessible singles like "Follow You, Follow Me"
  • Collins's trademark white Converse shoes and makeshift Top of the Pops setup with paint cans became iconic imagery
