Jesse Plemons Couldn’t Understand Why The ‘Breaking Bad’ Writers Were Afraid Of Him | People

Key Points

  • The actor did their own stunts, including being positioned on top of moving equipment
  • Reading the script was described as extremely stressful due to the intense content
  • Writers are praised for their incredible ability to weave information into exposition and stage directions
  • The script kept readers on edge and allowed them to visualize scenes clearly
  • There's a memorable scene involving a child character waving that helped resolve a situation
  • The actor had very limited character information initially - just a brief breakdown describing someone "fresh-faced, eager, innocuous but with something to hide"
  • The character's introduction involved helping others and giving a tip about a camera in a house
  • The actor felt unprepared and didn't understand their character when receiving new scripts
  • The actor actively sought information by cornering writers backstage for character insights
  • Writers remained secretive and wouldn't reveal character details
  • Other cast/crew members looked at the actor with fear, as if they were the character Todd
  • The actor was confused about why people seemed scared of them
  • Before shooting a crucial scene, the actor desperately asked director Vince for character guidance
  • Vince provided a metaphor about driving and encountering a raccoon in the road
  • The raccoon metaphor involved impossible choices: swerving right into a ditch or left into oncoming traffic
  • The metaphor represented the character's moral dilemmas and impossible situations
  • This raccoon metaphor served as the foundation for understanding Todd's character
  • The actor relied on this single piece of direction until the second season of the show
