Eli Manning goes undercover at Penn State walk-on tryouts as "Chad Powers"

Key Points

  • Penn State conducts walk-on tryouts twice a year - once in fall and once in spring
  • A disguised person named "Chad Powers" attempted to try out as a walk-on quarterback
  • Hollywood makeup artists were hired to create a convincing college student disguise
  • Chad Powers claimed to be "the next great Penn State quarterback"
  • There was debate about throwing strategy - short completions vs. deep passes to showcase arm strength
  • Chad preferred throwing deep passes despite advice to focus on chain-moving completions
  • Multiple receivers participated in the tryout to catch passes from quarterbacks
  • Chad demonstrated strong arm talent that attracted attention from other players
  • Word spread quickly about Chad's throwing ability, drawing crowds to watch
  • Chad consistently threw accurate passes that left marks on receivers' chests
  • Coaches and players were impressed by Chad's footwork and anticipation
  • Someone expressed interest in "signing the Chad Powers guy"
  • The coaching staff appreciates the walk-on program as an opportunity for players to chase dreams
  • Chad was ultimately disqualified for two reasons: poor testing numbers and ineligibility
  • Chad ran a 5.49 forty-yard dash and had a 7'10" broad jump - both inadequate
  • Chad had no remaining college eligibility, making him ineligible to play
  • Chad revealed he was actually 41 years old, just wanting to play football again
  • Despite the deception, the experience was positive and fun for all participants
