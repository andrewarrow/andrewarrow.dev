Using codex GPT4.5 "xhigh model" $200/month pro plan

A short note on hitting the wall at exactly the wrong time and immediately seeing the point of the expensive plan.

  • Posted 3 Apr 2026
  • andrewarrow.dev

"Man I'm on the $20 a month plan and I never run out!" I said proudly at an LAGrind tech breakfast. That very day I hit the limit and I've never agreed to an upsell so fast in my life before.

The funny thing about usage limits is they look generous right up until the moment your real workflow depends on them. When the tool is casual, the ceiling feels theoretical. When it is the thing helping you write, debug, plan, and untangle a mess in real time, the ceiling stops being a billing detail and starts being a productivity tax.

That is where the codex GPT4.5 xhigh model clicked for me. I do not need max reasoning for every prompt. I need it for the annoying, high-context, half-broken situations where I want the model to stay in the problem longer and actually push through the ambiguity instead of handing me the first clean-looking answer.

Two hundred dollars a month still sounds absurd if you say it slowly. It sounds less absurd when the alternative is burning time on resets, rationing prompts, or splitting work into smaller pieces just because your plan tapped out before your project did.

So yes, I upgraded fast. Not because I suddenly love premium pricing, but because I finally had a day where the cheap tier interrupted real work. That is usually when an upsell stops feeling like marketing and starts feeling like operations.
